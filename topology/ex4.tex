\section*{Problem 1}
Let \(\varphi: X_0 \subset X \longrightarrow Y\) be a continuous map. Recall the construction of glueing \(X\) to \(Y\) via \(\varphi\) from class.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Show that \(Y\) can be considered to be a subspace of \(X \cup_\varphi Y\). Show that the same is true for \(X\) if \(\varphi\) is a homeomorphism onto its image.
    \begin{proof}
        It is
        \begin{equation*}
            X \cup_\varphi Y := (X \sqcup Y) / \sim
        \end{equation*}
    \end{proof}
    \(a \sim f(a)\)

    We want to find a surjective map \(\psi: Y \longrightarrow X \cup_\varphi Y\) such that \(\psi(U)\) is open in the quotient topology.
\end{enumerate}
